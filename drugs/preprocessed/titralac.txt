No COMMON side effects have been reported with calcium carbonate (the active ingredient contained in Titralac) chewable tablets.
Seek medical attention right away if any of these SEVERE side effects occur while taking calcium carbonate:
Severe allergic reactions (rash; hives; itching; difficulty breathing; tightness in the chest; swelling of the mouth, face, lips, or tongue).
Uncommon ( to ): HypercalcemiaVery rare (less than ): Loss of appetite, milk-alkali syndromeFrequency not reported: Alkalosis
Uncommon ( to ): HypercalciuriaVery rare (less than ): Urinary frequency
Rare ( to ): Nausea, diarrhea, constipation, flatulence, eructation, dyspepsia, gastric pain, abdominal pain/discomfortVery rare (less than ): Vomiting
Very rare (less than ): Pruritus, rash, urticaria
Very rare (less than ): Renal impairment
Very rare (less than ): Continuous headache
Frequency not reported: Allergic reactions, hypersensitivity, anaphylactic reaction, angioedema
Frequency not reported: Unusual tiredness or weakness
Frequency not reported: Asthma
Frequency not reported: Muscular weakness
It is possible that some side effects of Titralac may not have been reported.
These can be reported to the FDA here.
Always consult a healthcare professional for medical advice.
