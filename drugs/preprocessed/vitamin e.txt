As well as its needed effects, vitamin e may cause unwanted side effects that require medical attention.
If any of the following side effects occur while taking vitamin e, check with your doctor or nurse as soon as possible:
Gastrointestinal side effects associated with oral doses greater than  units daily have rarely included nausea, diarrhea, and intestinal cramps.
Other side effects associated with oral doses greater than  units daily have rarely included fatigue, emotional disturbances, weakness, and headache.
Hematologic side effects associated with oral doses greater than  units daily have rarely included thrombophlebitis.
Ocular side effects associated with oral doses greater than  units daily have rarely included blurred vision.
Dermatologic side effects associated with oral doses greater than  units daily have rarely included rash.
Dermatologic side effects associated with topical application of vitamin E have included growth of white hair at site of alopecia and contact dermatitis.
Genitourinary side effects associated with oral doses greater than  units daily have rarely included gonadal dysfunction and breast soreness.
Endocrine side effects associated with oral doses greater than  units daily have rarely included decrease in serum thyroxine and triiodothyronine.
Metabolic side effects associated with oral doses greater than  units daily have rarely included creatinuria, increased serum creatinine kinase, increased serum cholesterol and triglycerides and increased urinary estrogens and androgens.
It is possible that some side effects of vitamin e may not have been reported.
These can be reported to the FDA here.
Always consult a healthcare professional for medical advice.
