Angioedema of the face, lips, tongue, and/or larynx has been reported with fesoterodine.
In some cases angioedema occurred after the first dose.
Angioedema associated with upper airway swelling may be life-threatening.
If involvement of the tongue, hypopharynx, or larynx occurs, fesoterodine should be promptly discontinued and appropriate therapy and/or measures to ensure a patent airway should be promptly provided.
