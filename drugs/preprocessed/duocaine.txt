Adverse reactions following administration of this drug are similar in nature to those observed with other amide local anesthetic agents.
The most serious adverse reactions tend to be systemic in nature.
In general, these adverse reactions are dose-related and may result from high plasma levels caused by excessive dosage, rapid absorption, or inadvertent intravascular injection, or may result from a hypersensitivity, idiosyncrasy, or diminished tolerance on the part of the patient.
