Get emergency medical help if you have any of these signs of an allergic reaction while taking ethanolamine oleate:  hives; difficult breathing; swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or throat.
Tell your caregivers at once if you have a serious side effect such as:
cough or hiccups (may be a sign of fluid buildup around the lungs);
fever, chills, cough with yellow or green mucus;
stabbing chest pain, feeling short of breath; or
severe pain or burning in your upper stomach.
Less serious side effects of ethanolamine oleate may include:
low fever; or
trouble swallowing.
This is not a complete list of side effects and others may occur.
Call your doctor for medical advice about side effects.
Respiratory side effects have included pleural effusion/infiltration  and pneumonia .
Local side effects have included esophageal ulcer  and esophageal stricture .
Esophagitis, tearing of the esophagus, sloughing of the mucosa overlying the injected varix, ulceration, necrosis, and periesophageal abscess and perforation have also been reported.
General side effects have included pyrexia  and retrosternal pain .
Bacteremia has been reported rarely.
Hypersensitivity side effects have rarely included anaphylactic shock.
Renal side effects have included acute renal failure.
Acute renal failure with spontaneous recovery has been reported in  cases following injection of  to  mL of ethanolamine oleate.
Nervous system side effects have rarely included spinal cord paralysis due to occlusion of the anterior spinal artery.
Hematologic side effects have rarely included disseminated intravascular coagulation.
