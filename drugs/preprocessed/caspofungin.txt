Gastrointestinal side effects have included diarrhea (up to ), vomiting (up to ), nausea (up to ), and abdominal pain (up to ).
Anorexia, abdominal distension, upper abdominal pain, constipation, and dyspepsia have been reported in less than  of patients.
Pancreatitis has been reported during postmarketing experience.
Other side effects have included fever (up to ), infusion-related adverse reactions (defined as a systemic event, such as flushing, fever, chills, hypotension, hypertension, tachycardia, dyspnea, tachypnea, rash, or anaphylaxis; up to ), chills (up to ), septic shock (up to ), aspergillosis , peripheral edema (up to ), sepsis (up to ), mucosal inflammation (up to ), flu-like illness, malaise, pain, and warm sensation.
Asthenia, fatigue, edema, flushing, and bacteremia have been reported in less than  of patients.
Swelling has been reported during postmarketing experience.
Metabolic side effects have included decreased blood potassium (up to ), increased blood alkaline phosphatase (up to ), hypokalemia (up to ), decreased blood albumin (up to ), decreased blood magnesium (up to ), increased blood glucose , decreased total serum protein , and elevated serum calcium .
Decreased appetite, fluid overload, hypomagnesemia, hypercalcemia, and hyperglycemia have been reported in less than  of patients.

Respiratory side effects have included respiratory failure (up to ), cough (up to ), pneumonia (up to ), pleural effusion , dyspnea (up to ), and rales .

Cardiovascular side effects have included hypotension (up to ), tachycardia (up to ), and hypertension (up to ).
Arrhythmia, atrial fibrillation, bradycardia, cardiac arrest, and myocardial infarction have been reported in less than  of patients.
Reported elevations in ALT and AST were accompanied by the use of cyclosporine in some of the patients enrolled in the clinical trials.

Nervous system side effects have included headache (up to ) and paresthesia ( to ).
