As well as its needed effects, oxiconazole topical (the active ingredient contained in Oxistat) may cause unwanted side effects that require medical attention.
Some oxiconazole topical side effects may not need any medical attention.
As your body gets used to the medicine these side effects may disappear.
Your health care professional may be able to help you prevent or reduce these side effects, but do check with them if any of the following side effects continue, or if you are concerned about them:
Cream:-Common ( to ): Pruritus, burning-Uncommon ( to ): Irritation, allergic contact dermatitis, folliculitis, erythema, papules, fissure, maceration, rashLotion:-Uncommon ( to ): Burning, pruritus, scaling, dyshidrotic eczema
Cream:-Uncommon ( to ): Stinging, nodulesLotion:-Uncommon ( to ): Stinging, pain
Lotion:-Uncommon ( to ): Tingling
It is possible that some side effects of Oxistat may not have been reported.
These can be reported to the FDA here.
