Side effects include:
Hot flashes, sleep disturbance, breast enlargement, breast pain or nipple tenderness, pain, back pain, constipation, peripheral edema, dizziness, headache, upper respiratory tract infection, diarrhea, dysuria, fatigue, frequent urination, nausea, urinary retention, urinary tract infection.
Endocrine side effects have included hot flushes , sleep disturbance , breast enlargement , and breast pain/nipple tenderness  as pharmacological consequences of androgen deprivation.
General side effects including pain  and fatigue  have been reported.
Musculoskeletal side effects including back pain  have been reported.
Physicians should carefully consider whether the risks of abarelix outweigh the benefits in patients with baseline QTc values > msec and in patients taking class IA or class III antiarrhythmic medications.
Cardiovascular side effects including peripheral edema  and prolongation of the QT interval have been reported.
Gastrointestinal side effects including constipation , diarrhea , and nausea  have been reported.
Nervous system side effects including dizziness  and headache  have been reported.
Respiratory side effects including upper respiratory tract infection  have been reported.
Genitourinary side effects including dysuria , micturition frequency , urinary retention , and urinary tract infection  have been reported.
Hypersensitivity side effects including immediate-onset allergic reactions , sometimes resulting in hypotension and syncope, have been reported to have occurred after administration of abarelix.
The allergic reaction included urticaria, pruritus, hypotension, and syncope.
Immediate-onset reactions have been reported to have occurred following any administration of abarelix, including the initial dose.
The cumulative risk for such a reaction increases with the duration of treatment.
Following each injection of abarelix, patients should be observed for at least  minutes in the office.
If an allergic reaction associated with hypotension and/or syncope does occur, supportive measures such as leg elevation, oxygen, IV fluids, antihistamines, corticosteroids, and epinephrine should be administered as needed.
Hepatic side effects including clinically meaningful increases in serum transaminases have been reported in a small percentage of patients.
