Common side effects of Daptacel (DTaP) include: fatigue, fever, gastrointestinal symptoms, headache, pain, erythema at injection site, and swelling at injection site.
See below for a comprehensive list of adverse effects.
If any of the following side effects occur while taking diphtheria and tetanus toxoids / pertussis, acellular, check with your doctor or nurse immediately:
Local side effects have included erythema, swelling, tenderness, and pain.
Hypersensitivity side effects have included rare reports of anaphylactic reactions (ie, hives, swelling of the mouth, difficulty breathing, hypotension, or shock).
Arthus-type hypersensitivity reactions, characterized by severe local reactions (generally starting 2 to 8 hours after an injection), may follow receipt of tetanus toxoid.
General side effects have included fever greater than 101 degrees F (rectal), high-pitched cry, persistent cry, and irritability.
Gastrointestinal side effects have included anorexia and vomiting.
Nervous system side effects have included drowsiness.
A review by the Institute of Medicine (IOM) found evidence for a causal relationship between tetanus toxoid and both brachial neuritis and Guillain-Barre syndrome.
