Uveitis generally occurs within  to  hours after administration of pamidronate (the active ingredient contained in Aredia)   The uveitis may be bilateral, and reversible, partial rd and th nerve palsies have been reported with the uveitis.
Recurrences have occurred with rechallenge of the drug.
Therapy with pamidronate should be discontinued if uveitis occurs.
Although severe cases have been reported, most symptoms have resolved on discontinuation of pamidronate with minimal or no intervention.
A nonspecific conjunctivitis has been reported with pamidronate use.
The conjunctivitis may consist of any or all of the following symptoms: conjunctival hyperemia, irritation, epiphora, discharge, and blurred vision.
These symptoms may appear within the first  to  hours after administration of the drug and generally resolve within a few days after drug discontinuation.
