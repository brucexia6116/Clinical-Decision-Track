Common side effects of Gilotrif include: diarrhea, abnormal hepatic function tests, acneiform eruption, skin rash, and erythema. Other side effects include: palmar-plantar erythrodysesthesia, and renal insufficiency.  See below for a comprehensive list of adverse effects.
The most frequent serious adverse reactions reported were diarrhea (6.6%); vomiting (4.8%); and dyspnea, fatigue, and hypokalemia (1.7% each).  Fatal adverse reactions included pulmonary toxicity/ILD-like adverse reactions (1.3%), sepsis (0.43%), and pneumonia (0.43%).  Dose reductions due to adverse reactions were required in 57% of patients.  The most frequent adverse reactions that led to dose reduction were diarrhea (20%), rash/acne (19%), paronychia (14%), and stomatitis (10%).  Discontinuation of therapy for adverse reactions was 14.0%.  The most frequent adverse reactions that led to discontinuation were diarrhea (1.3%), ILD (0.9%), and paronychia (0.9%).
