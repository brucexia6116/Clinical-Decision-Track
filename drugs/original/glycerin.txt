 Check with your doctor if any of these most COMMON side effects persist or become bothersome:
Anal irritation; burning sensation; diarrhea; gas; nausea; stomach cramps.
Severe allergic reactions (rash; hives; difficulty breathing; tightness in the chest; swelling of the mouth, face, lips, or tongue); rectal bleeding.
Gastrointestinal side effects associated with rectal administration have included rectal discomfort, irritation, burning or griping, cramping pain, tenesmus, abdominal discomfort, and diarrhea.  Hyperemia of the rectal mucosa with minimal amounts of hemorrhage and mucus discharge has been reported.
