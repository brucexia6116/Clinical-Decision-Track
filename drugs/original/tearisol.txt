Generic Name: hydroxypropyl methylcellulose ophthalmic
Applies to hydroxypropyl methylcellulose ophthalmic: intraocular solution
As well as its needed effects, hydroxypropyl methylcellulose ophthalmic (the active ingredient contained in Tearisol) may cause unwanted side effects that require medical attention. 

If any of the following side effects occur while taking hydroxypropyl methylcellulose ophthalmic, check with your doctor or nurse immediately:


It is possible that some side effects of Tearisol may not have been reported. These can be reported to the FDA here. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical advice.

Blurred vision
change in vision
decreased vision
loss of vision


Pain in the eye
redness of the white part of the eye or inside of the eyelid
sensitivity of the eye to light
tearing of the eye
throbbing eye pain


Pooling of a whitish fluid visible on the colored part of the eye
