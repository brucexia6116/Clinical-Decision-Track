The most commonly reported adverse reactions have included headache, nasopharyngitis, sinusitis, pharyngolaryngeal pain, upper respiratory infection, arthralgia, nasal congestion, and back pain.
Uncommon (0.1% to 1%): Application site reaction, application site dryness
Cough, dysphonia, and dry throat were reported in less than 1% of patients and at a greater incidence than with placebo.  Paradoxical bronchospasm may be related to the drug, the excipient, or evaporation cooling in the case of metered dose inhalers.

During clinical trials, Candida albicans infections of the mouth and pharynx occurred 32 of 3038 patients (1%); the majority of infections occurred in patients receiving 320 mcg per day.  Most cases were mild to moderate.  Dry mouth and nausea were reported in less than 1% of patients and at a greater incidence than with placebo.
